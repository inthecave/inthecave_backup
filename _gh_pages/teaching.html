Introduction to Philosophy

An Introduction to "Big Questions" in philosophy, including what is the relationship between mind and body? Do we have free will? What is justice?

Introduction to Ethics

An Introduction to the philosophical study of morality, focusing on issues in normative ethics - what makes right acts right? - as well as applied ethical issues - what is the moral status of non-human animals?

Syllabus

Medical Ethics

This course focuses on ethical issues that arise in medicine and medical practice, with particular emphasis on the sorts of issues actual medical practitioners face.

Syllabus

Philosophy of Law

A thorough investigation of the philosophical underpinnings of the law and legal systems. Particular focus on big questions like What is Law? How ought judges interpret legal texts? Do we have an obligation to follow the law?

Syllabus

Computers and Philosophy

Computers and the internet raise all sorts of philosophical issues - advanced computing makes us question the nature of intelligence, the collaborative tools of the internet allow us to consider the necessity of various social institutions. This course is often organized around those relevant issues that the students find of particular interest.
